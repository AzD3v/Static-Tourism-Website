Lagoons of Sete Cidades

The boiler where the lagoon is located was formed by successive collapses of two reliefs that surrounds it. It's one of the bigger boilers of the azorean archipelago. It's borders are, in their vast majority, very inclined slopes.

The lagoon is located at an montain area of quite inclinated relief, with interior cliffs, deep ravines and furrows where torrential waters flow. The Pico das Éguas, with 873 meters of altitude, it's the highest elevation of the area. The area around the lagoon includes an urban venue, agricultural land and locations of cryptomeria production.

The lagoon of Sete Cidades is one of the major reservatory of natural fresh water of the Azores, ocuping an vast area which aproaches approximately 4,35 km², with a profundity of 33 meters. The main reason that makes the lagoon so famous is it's double coloration, being divided by an shallow channel crossed over by an low bridge which separates waters of greenish colors on one side and bluish colors on the others. This characteristics and the beauty of the envolving landscape, gave rise to various legends (see Legends) about it's origin and creation, including one that connects the lagoon the the myth of the Atlantis. The lagoon of Sete Cidades and it's surroundings are classified as a protected landscape and one of the 7 natural wonders of Portugal.



The Fauna e flora of the lagoons

The lagoons are an important area of endemism (ocurrence of an specie or organism in an restric geographic area), conserving primitive remnants of the archipelago's vegetation, featuring the cedar-wood ("Juniperus brevifolia"), the "Chaerophyllum azoricum", the angelica ("Angelica lignescens"), the holly ("Ilex perado ssp. azorica"), the "Tolpis azorica", the ling ("Daboecia azorica"), the heather ("Erica azorica"), the "Lysimachia azorica", the sierra-grape ("Vaccinium cylindraceum"), the puff pastry ("Viburnum tinus ssp. subcordatum"), the "Cardamine caldeirarum", the daisies ("Beilis azorica"), as well as the mosses ("Breutelia azorica"), "Campylopus azoricus" and "Grimmia tricophylla ssp. azorica".

The lagoons also represent an important zone of passage to the migratory birds (a lot of them in danger of extinction). There are also azorean endemic birds, like the azorean pigeon-wood ("Columba palumbus azorica"), the blackbird ("Turdus merula azorensis") and the "estrelinha" ("Regulus regulus azoricus"). On the lagoons's waters there are various species of fish introducted by the Man, for example the the carp ("Cyprinus carpio"), the pike ("Esox lucius"), the "perca" ("Perca fluviatilis"), the red fish ("Rutílus rutílus") and the trout ("Salmo iridens gibrons").
