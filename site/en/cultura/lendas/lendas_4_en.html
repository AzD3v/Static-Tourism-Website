Legends of Sete Cidades

Legend of Sete Cidades, "Terra de Atlantes"

This legend tells the story of the princess Eufémia, daughter of the king Atlas and granddaughter of the god Jupiter. This princess had a beautiful and pure soul, who loved her freedom and because of that she refused the marriage arranged by your father with the son of the god Neptuno.

Neptuno became very offended with this refusal and ordered to destroy the kingdom of her father, causing the death of the princess. According to the myth, in her postmortem life, the princess converted to the christian faith and wished an return to the mortal world to spread good around it. Her wish was granted. When she returned, she was placed on an island where someone in a very ancient past had named Sete Cidades. It was a very poor place, where the people lived in great misery and pain. With the arrival of the princess, soon things started to change the quality of life at the island improved, leading to the disappearance of every single problem.

It’s oral tradition to assume that the princess still lives in the boiler inside the big volcano that gave birth to the Valley of the Sete Cidades, embodied in a beautiful solanaceae (a kind of plant that gives orange juice and whose leaves have excellent medicinal application. The legend says that “drinking this spiritual fluid is the cure of every sorrow”.
